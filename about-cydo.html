IN SERVICE FOR THE YOUTH

Welcome to Santa Rosa City Youth Development Office

Based on the Sangguniang Kabataan Reform Act of 2015 (RA 10742) and its Implementing Rules and Regulations, the Philippines mandates the establishment of a Local Youth Development Office (LYDO) in every province, city, and municipality. The Santa Rosa City Youth Development Office (SRYDO) is responsible for the comprehensive development and empowerment of the youth within the community.

Empowering Tomorrow's Leaders in Service for the Youth.

To ensure wide and multi-sectoral youth participation in local governance, a Local Youth Development Council (LYDC) is also established at each level, composed of representatives from youth and youth-serving organizations. The LYDC assists in planning and executing projects and programs of the LYDO, Sangguniang Kabataan, and the Pederasyon.

Who is CYDO?

The Santa Rosa City Youth Development Office (CYDO) was established in 2018 with the mission of supporting the city's youth. Though the pandemic initially hindered its visibility, the continuous efforts of the city government have since made CYDO well-known across Santa Rosa City. Today, the office is recognized for its dedication to empowering young people through various programs and initiatives aimed at personal and professional growth. CYDO plays a vital role in shaping future leaders and fostering community engagement among the youth.

City Youth Development Council is part of CYDO consisting of presidents from various registered youth organizations in Santa Rosa City. Each president has unique tasks, contributing to the council's diverse leadership. The organizations are assigned specific Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and are responsible for creating programs that target these goals. This approach ensures that each organization focuses on different areas like education, health, and sustainability, fostering a broad impact on the community. The CYDC's collaborative efforts empower young leaders and play a crucial role in supporting the city's youth development.

City Youth Development Plan is a project that is composed of ideas from different council members. CYDO takes these innovative ideas and develops a comprehensive training plan for city-wide implementation. The plan includes workshops, skill-building sessions, and community engagement initiatives, focusing on key areas like education and leadership development. By fostering collaboration between organizations, the plan aims to empower Santa Rosa's youth, equipping them with essential skills and encouraging active community participation. Through this initiative, CYDO is committed to nurturing future leaders and making a positive impact on the city.
